§1 Venue Overview

B3 S.A. — Brasil, Bolsa, Balcão (formerly BM&FBovespa) is Brazil's sole national exchange and the largest exchange in Latin America by market capitalisation. It operates two principal market segments relevant to BMLL derivatives coverage:

Trading Platform

B3 runs the PUMA Trading System (matching engine) for all exchange products. The Binary UMDF feed is the high-performance SBE-encoded market data dissemination layer built on top of PUMA. It operates alongside the legacy FIX/FAST UMDF feed (which continues to support MBP/TOB data not available in Binary UMDF).

Channel & Stream Architecture

Synchronisation Procedure (Simplified)

  1. Join Incremental stream → queue all messages
  2. Join Instrument Definition stream → build instrument table (wait for all TotNoRelatedSym)
  3. Join Snapshot Recovery → build books per instrument (wait for all TotNumReports)
  4. Apply queued incremental messages (discard those ≤ LastMsgSeqNumProcessed from snapshots)
  5. Unjoin Snapshot and Instrument Definition streams
  6. Continue normal processing from Incremental only
⚠️ RptSeq per instrument: The field RptSeq (tag 83) is a sequence number per instrument (not per packet). Gaps in RptSeq for a specific SecurityID indicate lost data for that instrument even if no packet-level gaps occurred. This is the preferred gap-detection mechanism when monitoring a subset of instruments.
⚠️ Not in Binary UMDF: Settlement prices (tag 269=6), Open Interest (tag 269=C), and Notional Volume (tag 269=B) were added in v1.8.0 as dedicated SettlementPrice (template 28) and OpenInterest (template 29) messages. These were previously unavailable in Binary UMDF (only in legacy FIX/FAST). Confirm historical availability with engineering.

§3 Instrument Reference Data

Instrument definitions arrive via the SecurityDefinition message on the Instrument Definition Stream. One message per instrument; packet may contain multiple. Updates (e.g. new/expired instruments intraday) come via the Incremental Stream.

ℹ️ The Instrument Definition Stream is not restarted daily — it is brought down only between Fri 22:00 and Sun 12:00 local time (São Paulo). Subscribers may stay connected through the week.

§8 Exchange Nuances

8.1 Iceberg Orders

B3 supports Disclosed Quantity (iceberg) orders. The displayed portion (MaxFloor, tag 111) is visible in the order book. When the displayed quantity is fully filled:

  • The order is replenished at the end of the price level queue (lowest priority), preserving hidden nature
  • A new secondaryOrderID (tag 198) is issued on replenishment — the old ID is deleted and a new one inserted
  • tradedHiddenQty in ExecutionSummary captures total hidden quantity matched in the event
⚠️ BMLL L3 processing: treat a new secondaryOrderID as a new order (DELETE + INSERT pair). Do not attempt to track iceberg replenishment as a MODIFY.

8.2 Order Modification and Priority Loss

In B3 MBO data, order modifications that cause priority loss (price change or quantity increase) appear as a DELETE of the old secondaryOrderID followed by a NEW insertion with a new secondaryOrderID. Only modifications that do not cause priority loss (quantity decrease) appear as a CHANGE update to the same position.

8.3 Spread / Strategy Trade Handling (LEG_TRADE)

When a strategy (spread) trade occurs, B3 publishes:

  1. A Trade on the strategy instrument (with trdSubType=102, LEG_TRADE)
  2. Separate Trades on each underlying leg instrument
⚠️ Double-counting risk: To avoid double-counting volume, set PRINTABLE=false on leg trades where the strategy-level trade has already been counted. The exact PRINTABLE logic for leg trades requires engineering confirmation — the spec defines them as non-regular but does not specify a definitive double-count flag.

8.4 Multi-Asset Trades (MULTI_ASSET_TRADE)

trdSubType=101 (MULTI_ASSET_TRADE) indicates a trade in the cash instrument originated from a forward + cash agreement (Termo Vista). It is a non-regular trade but is NOT a block trade. Associated with a forward Termo contract trade on a separate instrument.

8.5 Settlement Price Precision

Closing prices in Binary UMDF use a different decimal precision from other prices:

  • Most prices: exponent = -4 (4 decimal places)
  • ClosingPrice.mDEntryPx and netChgPrevDay: exponent = -8 (8 decimal places) — these can be adjusted for corporate events and may require higher precision

8.8 Self-Trade Prevention (STP)

B3 supports STP at customer level. When triggered, the cancelled order quantity appears in cxlQty of the ExecutionSummary. The cancellation itself is published as a DeleteOrder_MBO before the Trade message in the event sequence. Three STP modes: cancel aggressor (default), cancel resting, cancel both.

8.9 Stop Orders

Stop order entry, election, and cancellation of unelected stop orders do NOT generate market data messages. Only the triggered trade (after election) generates market data. Multiple stop orders elected in a single matching event all belong to one ExecutionSummary event with one combined summary and multiple Trade messages.

8.10 Trading Hours / DST

PUMA DRV is not restarted daily. The platform runs continuously from Sunday 12:00 to Friday 22:00 São Paulo local time. B3 recommends subscribers disconnect during weekends unless participating in mock tests. São Paulo observes DST (Horário de Brasília): UTC-3 in winter, UTC-2 in summer. Transition dates differ from US/European DST calendars.

§9 Order Book Model

Position-Number Based MBO

B3's order book is internally maintained by price-level and priority, but the market data diffusion uses position numbers (mDEntryPositionNo, tag 290) as the external representation. Position 1 = best price. Client systems MUST manage books using position numbers, not derived price levels.

Key rule: When an order is inserted at position N, all existing orders at positions N through the end of the book shift down by 1. B3 does NOT send change messages for the shifted orders. The receiver must infer the shift.

Market Orders

Market orders appear in the book with mDEntryPx = null (0x8000000000000000). They have position numbers and a secondaryOrderID but no price. In BMLL L3, store price as NaN.
